Research Statement

In metal additive manufacturing (MAM), the metal (typically in powder form) is repeated melted by an energy source (e.g., laser) and solidified (when disengaged from the laser) to become the 3-dimensional build. My research is to simulate the physical phenomena in this process with computational techniques. The simualtion, by "reproduce" the physical phenomena, can contribute to the maturation of MAM in two ways: (1) the underlying science can be quantitatively understood, which informs better process design. (2) the simulation can partially replace the trial-and-error tests to expediate the optimization of process parameters.

Powder Bed Generation and Ray Tracing

This project is aimed to simulate the laser absorption distribution among a powder bed, as typically in a laser powder bed fusion process. A randomly packed powder bed is generated by the PACKS algorithm (stands for Probabilistic-Analytic Consecutive Kinetic Simulation). The powder bed is then represented by the level-set function. The ray-tracing algorithm is implemented to calculate the absorbed distribution.
